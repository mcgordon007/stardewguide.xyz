# Trinkets Guide

**Trinkets** are equippable accessories introduced in Stardew Valley 1.6. Each trinket provides unique passive benefits to combat. They occupy their own **dedicated equipment slot**, separate from your two ring slots, allowing you to stack three different accessories at once (two rings + one trinket).

Trinkets are unlocked after you claim **Combat Mastery** at the Mastery Cave. After unlocking, most trinkets can drop from monsters, crates and barrels in the Mines, Skull Cavern, and Quarry Mine. The Magic Hair Gel is an exception that can only be purchased during the Desert Festival.

## TL;DR

Trinkets are new 1.6 combat accessories in a dedicated slot. Obtain them after Combat Mastery, most from monster drops. 8 total trinkets exist: 5 combat-focused, 2 cosmetic, 1 unique magic arrow effect. You can re-roll trinket stats on the Anvil for 3 Iridium Bars.

## How Trinkets Work

### Basic Mechanics

| Mechanic | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Equipment Slot** | Dedicated trinket slot (separate from 2 rings) |
| **Unlocked By** | Claiming Combat Mastery at Mastery Cave |
| **Sources** | Monster drops, Skull Cavern treasure rooms, crates, Desert Festival (Magic Hair Gel) |
| **Stacking** | One trinket equipped at a time |
| **Re-forging** | Can re-roll stats on the Anvil (3 Iridium Bars per roll) |
| **Sell Price** | 1,000g each at Adventurer's Guild |

### Drop Chances

Trinkets drop randomly from monsters and containers:

- **Monster Drops**: All monsters have a chance (varies by monster type and health)
- **Skull Cavern Treasure Chests**: ~0.63% chance
- **Crates/Barrels**: 0.4% + chance with luck bonuses
- Luck buffs (Daily Luck + Luck Rings) increase drop chances
- Burglar's Ring and Monster Compendium **do not affect** trinket drop rates

### Basilisk Paw

**Effect:** Grants full immunity to all debuffs from enemies (e.g., slowness, damage over time, chilled, burning)

**Source:** Random drops from monsters and crates

**Best For:** Deep Skull Cavern exploration where many enemies apply debuffs. The immunity prevents you from being permanently slowed or poisoned, making it extremely valuable for long dungeon runs.

---

### Fairy Box

**Effect:** Summons a fairy companion that periodically heals you in combat

**Stats:** Level 1-5; higher level = stronger healing

**Source:** Random drops from monsters and crates

**Best For:** Survival-focused gameplay in the Skull Cavern and Volcano Dungeon. Combine with Vampire Ring for near-infinite sustain.

**Strategy Tip:** Fairy Box healing procs periodically - don't rely on it as your only source of healing. Always carry emergency food.

---

### Frog Egg

**Effect:** Summons a frog pet companion that follows you around.

**Mechanics:** Enemies eaten by the frog **do not drop loot or count towards kills/quests**. The frog only eats small enemies.

**Source:** Random drops from monsters and crates

**Best For:** Purely cosmetic companion. It doesn't provide combat benefits, just adds atmosphere to your adventures.

---

### Golden Spur

**Effect:** When you land a critical hit, you gain an 8-second speed boost.

**Duration:** 8 seconds (maximum)

**Source:** Random drops from monsters and crates

**Best For:** High critical chance builds. Combine with the Scout profession + high critical weapons for frequent speed boosts. Makes kiting easier after engaging groups of enemies.

---

### Ice Rod

**Effect:** Shoots an orb of ice every X seconds that freezes any enemies hit, stopping their movement for a few seconds.

**Stats:** Frequency 3-4 seconds; duration 3-4 seconds. Higher trinket level improves frequency and duration.

**Source:** Random drops from monsters and crates

**Best For:** Crowd control in the Mines and Skull Cavern. Freezing large groups of enemies makes them easier to handle one at a time.

---

### Magic Hair Gel

**Effect:** Your hair continuously cycles through all rainbow colors.

**Source:** Only obtainable by purchasing from Alex's shop during the [Desert Festival](https://stardewvalleywiki.com/Desert_Festival) (held Spring 15-17)

**Best For:** Purely cosmetic. It doesn't affect combat at all, just makes your character look cool.

---

### Magic Quiver

**Effect:** Shoots a magic arrow at nearby enemies every few seconds. Damage varies based on the type of Magic Quiver you get.

**Types:** Different affixes (Perfect, Rapid, Heavy) change arrow properties.

**Source:** Random drops from monsters and crates

**Notes:** Kills from magic arrows **do count** towards quests and monster eradication goals.

---

### Parrot Egg

**Effect:** Summons a parrot companion that gives you a chance to find extra gold when you slay monsters.

**Stats:** Level 1-4; higher level = higher gold chance (~15% at level 4)

**Source:** Random drops from monsters and crates

**Best For:** Skull Cavern farming runs. The extra gold adds up significantly during extended dungeon dives.

---

## Mastery Cave Unlocking

To access trinkets, you must:

1. Reach **level 10 in Combat skill**
2. Visit the **Mastery Cave** (unlocked after all skills reach level 10)
3. Claim your **Combat Mastery** reward
4. After this, trinkets start dropping from monsters

## Re-forging Trinkets

All trinkets except **Basilisk Paw** and **Magic Hair Gel** can be **re-forged** on the Anvil in the Volcano Dungeon. Re-forging:

- Randomizes the trinket's stats (level for most, type for Magic Quiver)
- Costs 3 **Iridium Bars** per re-roll
- You can re-roll as many times as you want until you get perfect stats

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I wear a trinket and two rings at the same time?

**Yes!** The trinket slot is completely separate from the two ring slots. You can wear one trinket + two rings simultaneously.

### Do trinkets break or need repair?

No, trinkets have infinite durability and never break.

### Can I get the same trinket multiple times?

Yes, you can get duplicate trinkets. Duplicates can be sold for 1,000g each at the Adventurer's Guild.

### Where can I find the highest drop rates?

- **Skull Cavern treasure rooms** have the highest guaranteed chance (~0.63%)
- **Deep in the Mines** (level 80+) monsters have higher drop chances
- **Luck buffs** increase drop chances significantly - use Lucky Lunch and wear a Luck Ring before trinket hunting
