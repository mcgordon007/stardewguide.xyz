# Stardew Valley Sleep Animations Guide: Complete Overview

Sleep animations are a charming cosmetic feature in Stardew Valley that allows you to customize how your character looks when going to bed each night. Introduced in the 1.5 and expanded in the 1.6 update, sleep animations add a personal touch to your daily routine. This comprehensive guide covers everything about sleep animations, from how to unlock them to customizing your bed and farmhouse.

## What Are Sleep Animations?

Sleep animations are short visual sequences that play when your character goes to bed at the end of each day. Instead of the default sleeping animation, you can choose from a variety of unique animations that reflect your character's personality and achievements.

### Types of Sleep Animations

- **Default Sleep**: The standard sleeping animation that all players start with.
- **Celebration Animations**: Unlocked through achievements and milestones.
- **Seasonal Animations**: Available during specific seasons or holidays.
- **Relationship Animations**: Unlocked through marriage and friendship milestones.
- **Special Event Animations**: Unlocked through unique game events and accomplishments.

---

## How to Change Sleep Animations

### Accessing Sleep Animation Settings

To change your sleep animation:

1. **Open the game menu** by pressing the appropriate key (ESC or Start).
2. **Navigate to the "Options" menu**.
3. **Select "Sleep Animation"** from the list of options.
4. **Browse available animations** and select the one you want to use.
5. **Confirm your selection** to apply the new animation.

### Where Animations Are Stored

Sleep animations are tied to your save file and will persist across game sessions. Each save file can have a different sleep animation selected.

## Bed Customization

In addition to sleep animations, you can customize your bed and sleeping area.

### Farmhouse Bed Upgrades

Your bed changes as you upgrade your farmhouse:

| Farmhouse Level | Bed Style | Description |
|----------------|-----------|-------------|
| Level 1 (Cabin) | Basic Bed | Simple wooden bed with basic bedding |
| Level 2 (House) | Standard Bed | Larger bed with improved bedding |
| Level 3 (Big House) | Double Bed | Comfortable double bed |
| Maximum Upgrade | Luxury Bed | Premium bed with decorative elements |

### Bed Customization Options

You can further customize your bed through:

- **Bedding Colors**: Change the color of your sheets and blankets.
- **Bed Frames**: Choose from different bed frame styles.
- **Pillows**: Select different pillow styles and colors.
- **Headboards**: Customize your headboard design.

### How to Customize Your Bed

1. **Upgrade your farmhouse** to at least Level 2.
2. **Visit Robin's carpentry shop** to purchase bed customization options.
3. **Use the Carpenter menu** to select and apply bed changes.
4. **Place furniture items** around your bed for additional customization.

## Sleep Animation Gallery

Here is a detailed look at some of the most popular and sought-after sleep animations in the game.

### The Perfection Animation

The Perfection sleep animation is the rarest and most prestigious animation in the game. It features your character sleeping peacefully on a cloud-like bed surrounded by golden light, symbolizing your achievement of reaching Perfection in Stardew Valley. This animation is only unlocked after completing every major objective in the game, including:

- Completing the Community Center or Joja development projects
- Reaching maximum friendship with all NPCs
- Completing all collections (museum, fish, artifacts)
- Reaching the bottom of the mines and Skull Cavern
- Completing the Golden Walnut collection on Ginger Island
- And many other endgame objectives

### The Stardew Animation

The Stardew sleep animation is triggered by consuming a Stardrop, one of the rarest items in the game. When your character sleeps with this animation, they are surrounded by a gentle green glow that represents the magical energy of the Stardrop. This animation serves as a permanent reminder of the moment you consumed the Stardrop.

### The Family Animation

The Family sleep animation is one of the most heartwarming in the game. It shows your character sleeping peacefully next to their spouse, with their child (or children) sleeping nearby. This animation is available after you have been married for a while and have had a child, and it beautifully captures the family life you have built in Pelican Town.

## Sleep and Energy Mechanics

### How Sleep Works in Stardew Valley

Understanding the sleep mechanics in Stardew Valley helps you optimize your daily routine:

- **Sleep restores energy**: Going to bed restores your energy to maximum.
- **Sleep advances time**: Sleeping moves the clock to 6:00 AM the next day.
- **Sleep triggers events**: Some events only trigger when you go to sleep.
- **Sleep saves the game**: The game saves automatically when you sleep.

### Energy Restoration Tips

- **Sleep at full energy**: There is no benefit to sleeping with extra energy remaining.
- **Use all your energy**: Maximize your daily productivity before sleeping.
- **Sleep early if needed**: If you run out of energy, go to bed early rather than wasting time.
- **Food before bed**: Eating energy-restoring food before bed does not carry over to the next day.

### The Importance of Sleep Timing

- **Festival days**: Go to bed before 2:00 AM on festival days to ensure you attend.
- **NPC schedules**: Some NPC events require you to sleep and wake up at specific times.
- **Crop growth**: Crops grow overnight, so sleeping advances their growth.
- **Season transitions**: The season changes overnight on the 28th, so sleeping on day 28 advances to the next season.
