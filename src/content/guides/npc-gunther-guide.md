# Stardew Valley Gunther Guide: Complete NPC Overview

> **IMPORTANT: Gunther is NOT a giftable NPC.** You cannot give gifts to Gunther, build friendship with him, or trigger heart events. He has no birthday and no relationship mechanics. The sections below about gift preferences, heart events, and birthday are fictional and do not exist in the game. Gunther's only role is as the Museum curator who accepts donations.

Gunther is the curator of the Pelican Town Museum, also known as the Library and Museum or the Archaeology House. As the guardian of the town's collection of artifacts, minerals, and fossils, Gunther plays a crucial role in one of the game's major objectives: completing the museum collection. This comprehensive guide covers everything about Gunther's role as curator and the rewards for museum donations.

## Basic Information

### Character Overview

**Name**: Gunther
**Role**: Museum Curator

**Family**: None known

### Personality

Gunther is a scholarly, enthusiastic, and somewhat eccentric curator who is deeply passionate about history, archaeology, and natural sciences. He takes great pride in the museum's collection and is always excited to receive new donations. Despite his academic demeanor, Gunther is warm and friendly, often expressing genuine excitement when the player brings in rare or valuable items. He has a dry wit and occasionally makes self-deprecating jokes about the museum's limited funding and visitor traffic.

## Museum Donations

The core of Gunther's role in the game is managing museum donations. Donating items to the museum is one of the primary objectives in Stardew Valley.

### How to Donate

1. **Find an artifact or mineral** through mining, digging, fishing, or other activities.
2. **Bring the item to Gunther** at the museum during business hours.
3. **Select the "Donate" option** when talking to Gunther.
4. **Gunther will place the item** on display in the museum.

### Types of Donations

#### Minerals (53 total)

Minerals are found primarily through mining. Break rocks in the mines, open geodes at Clint's, or find them through other means. Key minerals include:

- Quartz, Earth Crystal, Frozen Tear, Fire Quartz
- Copper Ore, Iron Ore, Gold Ore, Iridium Ore
- Various gems including Amethyst, Aquamarine, Diamond, Emerald, Ruby, Topaz
- Rare minerals like Prismatic Shard

#### Artifacts (42 total)

Artifacts are found through digging, fishing, killing monsters, and other activities. Key artifacts include:

- Dwarf Scroll I-IV, Dwarvish artifacts
- Ancient Doll, Ancient Drum, Ancient Sword
- Chipped Amphora, Arrowhead, Bone Flute
- Ornamental Fan, Prehistoric Handaxe, Prehistoric Scapula
- Rare artifacts like the Ancient Seed and Elvish Jewelry

#### Special Collections

- **Dwarf Collection**: Four Dwarf-related artifacts that unlock the ability to understand the Dwarf.
- **Skeleton Collection**: Multiple skeleton pieces that form a complete display.
- **Fossil Collection**: Various prehistoric fossils including the complete set of dinosaur bones.

## Museum Completion

Completing the museum is one of the most satisfying long-term goals in Stardew Valley.

### Total Items Needed

- **53 Minerals**
- **42 Artifacts**
- **95 Total Items**

### Tips for Completing the Museum

1. **Mine regularly**: The mines are the primary source of minerals and some artifacts.
2. **Dig artifact spots**: Use your hoe on the small worms that appear on the ground, especially in winter.
3. **Open geodes**: Process geodes at Clint's blacksmith shop to find minerals.
4. **Fish**: Some artifacts can only be found through fishing, particularly in specific locations and seasons.
5. **Kill monsters**: Certain artifacts drop from specific monsters in the mines and Skull Cavern.
6. **Check the Traveling Cart**: The Traveling Cart occasionally sells rare minerals and artifacts.
7. **Use the panning tool**: Pan in rivers and streams to find minerals and artifacts.
8. **Visit Ginger Island**: Several rare artifacts and minerals can be found on Ginger Island.

### Tracking Progress

- **Check the museum displays** to see which items you have already donated.
- **Use the collections tab** in the game menu to track your progress.
- **Gunther will tell you** how many items you have donated when you talk to him.
