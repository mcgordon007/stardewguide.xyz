# Stardew Valley Farm Buildings Guide: Complete Overview

Farm buildings are essential structures that expand your farming capabilities in Stardew Valley. From housing animals to processing crops to teleporting across the valley, each building serves a unique purpose. This comprehensive guide covers every farm building available, including costs, materials, purposes, and strategic considerations.

## Overview of Farm Buildings

Farm buildings are purchased and constructed by Robin at the Carpenter's Shop. Each building requires gold and specific materials. Construction takes 2-3 days depending on the building type. Buildings can be placed anywhere on your farm's buildable tiles and can be demolished or moved later.

### Building Categories

1. **Animal Housing**: Coops and Barns for livestock
2. **Utility Buildings**: Silo, Well, Mill
3. **Special Buildings**: Slime Hutch, Junimo Hut
4. **Magical Buildings**: Obelisks, Gold Clock
5. **Infrastructure**: Stable, Shipping Bin

## Coop Buildings

### Basic Coop

**Cost**: 4,000g
**Materials**: Wood (300), Stone (100)
**Size**: 6x3 tiles
**Capacity**: 4 animals
**Construction Time**: 2 days

**Purpose**: Houses chickens. The basic coop provides shelter for your poultry, protecting them from weather and allowing them to produce eggs.

**Features**:
- Basic feeding trough (manual feeding required)
- Simple incubator for hatching eggs
- No hay storage (requires silo)
- Animals can be let outside during good weather

**Animals**: Chickens

### Big Coop

**Cost**: 10,000g (upgrade from Basic Coop)
**Materials**: Wood (400), Stone (150)
**Size**: 6x3 tiles
**Capacity**: 8 animals
**Construction Time**: 2 days

**Purpose**: Upgraded coop with additional capacity and features.

**Features**:
- Larger feeding trough
- Improved incubator
- Can house ducks in addition to chickens
- Still requires manual feeding

**Animals**: Chickens, Ducks

### Deluxe Coop

**Cost**: 20,000g (upgrade from Big Coop)
**Materials**: Wood (500), Stone (200)
**Size**: 6x3 tiles
**Capacity**: 12 animals
**Construction Time**: 3 days

**Purpose**: The ultimate coop with automatic feeding and maximum capacity.

**Features**:
- Auto-feed system (pulls hay from silo automatically)
- Deluxe incubator
- Can house rabbits and dinosaurs
- Maximum efficiency for poultry farming

**Animals**: Chickens, Ducks, Rabbits, Dinosaurs, Golden Chickens (after perfection)

## Barn Buildings

### Basic Barn

**Cost**: 6,000g
**Materials**: Wood (350), Stone (150)
**Size**: 7x4 tiles
**Capacity**: 4 animals
**Construction Time**: 2 days

**Purpose**: Houses cows. Barns provide shelter for larger livestock.

**Features**:
- Basic feeding trough
- No hay storage (requires silo)
- Animals can graze outside during good weather
- Space for animal products

**Animals**: Cows

### Big Barn

**Cost**: 12,000g (upgrade from Basic Barn)
**Materials**: Wood (450), Stone (200)
**Size**: 7x4 tiles
**Capacity**: 8 animals
**Construction Time**: 2 days

**Purpose**: Upgraded barn with increased capacity.

**Features**:
- Larger feeding trough
- Can house goats (unlocked)
- Still requires manual feeding

**Animals**: Cows, Goats

### Deluxe Barn

**Cost**: 25,000g (upgrade from Big Barn)
**Materials**: Wood (550), Stone (300)
**Size**: 7x4 tiles
**Capacity**: 12 animals
**Construction Time**: 3 days

**Purpose**: The ultimate barn with automatic feeding.

**Features**:
- Auto-feed system
- Maximum capacity for large livestock
- Can house pigs (unlocked)
- Best efficiency for animal farming

**Animals**: Cows, Goats, Sheep, Pigs

## Slime Hutch

**Cost**: 10,000g
**Materials**: Stone (500), Refined Quartz (10)
**Size**: 11x6 tiles
**Capacity**: 20 slimes
**Construction Time**: 2 days

**Purpose**: Houses and breeds slimes for slime ball production.

**Features**:
- Four water troughs for slime hydration
- Slime press for producing slime balls
- Can breed different colored slimes
- Slimes produce slime balls when happy

**Slime Ball Production**:
- Slimes produce slime balls daily when well-fed
- Each slime ball contains 10-20 slime
- Slimes can be bred for different colors
- Purple slimes drop rare items occasionally

**Strategy Tips**:
- Keep slimes watered and happy
- Breed slimes for variety
- Collect slime balls regularly
- Use slime for crafting oil makers and other items

## Mill

**Cost**: 2,500g
**Materials**: Stone (50), Wood (150), Cloth (4)
**Size**: 4x2 tiles
**Construction Time**: 2 days

**Purpose**: Processes wheat and beets into flour and sugar.

**Features**:
- Converts wheat to flour (1 wheat = 1 flour)
- Converts beets to sugar (1 beet = 1 sugar)
- Automatic processing overnight
- Products appear in output box next morning

**Product Values**:
| Input | Output | Input Value | Output Value |
|-------|--------|-------------|--------------|
| Wheat | Flour | 30g | 100g |
| Beet | Sugar | 100g | 50g |

**Note**: Processing wheat to flour increases value significantly. Processing beets to sugar decreases value, so only do this if you need sugar for cooking.

## Silo

**Cost**: 100g
**Materials**: Stone (100), Clay (10), Copper Bar (5)
**Size**: 3x3 tiles
**Capacity**: 240 hay
**Construction Time**: 2 days

**Purpose**: Stores hay for feeding animals during winter and rainy days.

**Features**:
- Automatically collects hay when cutting grass with scythe
- Stores up to 240 hay per silo
- Hay can be retrieved from inside
- Essential for winter animal care

**Hay Management Tips**:
- Build silos before cutting grass to maximize collection
- Each grass tile cut produces 1 hay
- Multiple silos increase total storage
- Calculate winter needs: Animals x 28 days = hay needed
- Example: 12 animals need 336 hay for winter (2 silos)

## Well

**Cost**: 1,000g
**Materials**: Stone (75)
**Size**: 3x3 tiles
**Construction Time**: 2 days

**Purpose**: Provides unlimited water source for filling watering cans.

**Features**:
- Infinite water supply
- Convenient placement anywhere on farm
- Useful before sprinklers are available
- Aesthetic decoration

**Strategic Use**:
- Place near crop fields for early game convenience
- Becomes less useful once sprinklers are installed
- Still valuable for filling watering cans for greenhouse
- Good aesthetic addition to farm design

---

## Junimo Hut

**Cost**: 20,000g
**Materials**: Stone (200), Starfruit (9), Fiber (100)
**Size**: 3x2 tiles
**Unlock**: Complete Community Center or Joja route
**Construction Time**: 3 days

**Purpose**: Junimos automatically harvest crops within a 17x17 tile area.

**Features**:
- Automatic crop harvesting
- Coverage: 17x17 tiles (289 tiles)
- Junimos store harvested crops inside hut
- Works year-round including greenhouse

**Harvest Mechanics**:
- Junimos harvest when crops are ready
- They don't plant or water crops
- Harvested crops appear in hut inventory
- Junimos work faster with more huts nearby

**Optimal Placement**:
- Center of crop fields
- Align with sprinkler coverage
- Multiple huts for large fields
- Consider path access for collecting crops

## Obelisks

Obelisks provide instant teleportation to key locations. Each obelisk costs 500,000g and requires specific materials.

### Earth Obelisk

**Cost**: 500,000g
**Materials**: Hardwood (10), Iridium Bar (10), Earth Crystal (10)
**Size**: 3x2 tiles
**Destination**: Mountains (near Robin's house)

**Purpose**: Instant teleportation to the mountain area.

**Benefits**:
- Quick access to mines
- Near Carpenter's Shop
- Close to Linus's tent
- Saves travel time daily

### Water Obelisk

**Cost**: 500,000g
**Materials**: Hardwood (10), Iridium Bar (10), Clam (10)
**Size**: 3x2 tiles
**Destination**: Beach (near Willy's shop)

**Purpose**: Instant teleportation to the beach.

**Benefits**:
- Quick access to Willy's Fish Shop
- Ocean fishing location
- Near tide pools
- Saves travel time for fishing

### Desert Obelisk

**Cost**: 500,000g
**Materials**: Hardwood (10), Iridium Bar (10), Coconut (10)
**Size**: 3x2 tiles
**Destination**: Calico Desert

**Purpose**: Instant teleportation to the desert.

**Benefits**:
- No need for bus pass
- Quick access to Skull Cavern
- Near Sandy's shop
- Access to oasis and casino

### Island Obelisk

**Cost**: 500,000g
**Materials**: Hardwood (10), Iridium Bar (10), Banana (10)
**Size**: 3x2 tiles
**Destination**: Ginger Island (near dock)
**Unlock**: Complete Ginger Island parrot quest

**Purpose**: Instant teleportation to Ginger Island.

**Benefits**:
- Quick access to island farm
- Near island shop
- Access to volcano dungeon
- Saves boat travel time

## Gold Clock

**Cost**: 10,000,000g
**Materials**: Gold Bar (5), Iridium Bar (10)
**Size**: 3x2 tiles
**Unlock**: After completing Community Center or Joja route
**Construction Time**: 3 days

**Purpose**: Prevents debris spread and automatically closes gates.

**Features**:
- Stops weeds, rocks, and logs from appearing on farm
- Automatically closes all gates
- Permanent effect on entire farm
- Ultimate convenience item

**Benefits**:
- No daily debris cleanup
- Gates always closed (prevents animal escape)
- Perfectly maintained farm appearance
- Saves significant time daily

**Value Analysis**:
- Expensive but saves hours of gameplay time
- Essential for perfection achievement
- Worth prioritizing after other buildings
- Dramatically improves farm management

---

## Stable

**Cost**: 10,000g
**Materials**: Hardwood (100), Iron Bar (5)
**Size**: 2x2 tiles
**Construction Time**: 2 days

**Purpose**: Provides a horse for faster travel.

**Features**:
- Includes one horse automatically
- Horse provides +30% movement speed
- Horse can be ridden anywhere
- Stable stores horse when not riding

**Horse Benefits**:
- Faster travel across valley
- Can ride through most areas
- Horse returns to stable if left
- Essential for efficient daily routine

---

## Shipping Bin

**Cost**: Free (already on farm)
**Materials**: None
**Size**: 1x1 tile
**Purpose**: Sell items for gold.

**Features**:
- Items sold overnight
- 100% of base value (no penalty)
- Lewis collects items each night

**Note:** There is only one Shipping Bin on the farm. Additional Shipping Bins cannot be purchased or built in the game.

## Building Strategy Tips

### Priority Order

**Early Game**:
1. Silo (before winter)
2. Coop or Barn (choose based on preference)
3. Well (if no sprinklers)

**Mid Game**:
1. Upgrade animal buildings
2. Mill (if growing wheat)
3. Stable (for horse)

**Late Game**:
1. Junimo Huts (after Community Center)
2. Obelisks (one at a time)
3. Gold Clock (final priority)

### Space Planning

- Reserve space for late-game buildings
- Plan animal district layout
- Consider Junimo Hut coverage
- Leave room for obelisks near house

### Animal Building Tips

- Start with one building type
- Upgrade before expanding
- Build silos early
- Plan grazing areas
