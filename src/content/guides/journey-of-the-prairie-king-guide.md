# Journey of the Prairie King Guide

**Journey of the Prairie King** is a SmashTV-style top-down shooter minigame found inside the **Stardrop Saloon**. It is one of two arcade machines available in the saloon and offers a challenging arcade experience with multiple stages, power-ups, and bosses.

## Unlock

Journey of the Prairie King is **available from the start** of the game. No unlock is required. Simply head to the **Stardrop Saloon** and interact with the arcade machine to begin playing.

Additionally, **Abigail** has a console version of the game that is playable during her **2-Heart Event**.

## Controls

### PC (Keyboard)
- **WASD** — Move your character
- **Arrow Keys** — Shoot in the corresponding direction
- You can move and shoot diagonally by combining two keys (for example, W + Up Arrow moves and shoots diagonally up-left)

### Controller
- **Left Joystick** — Move
- **Right Joystick** — Shoot
- **D-Pad** — Can also be used for shooting

### Mobile
- **Two transparent red circles** appear on screen:
  - Left circle — Move
  - Right circle — Aim and shoot

## Lives

You begin the game with **4 lives**, displayed as **"x3"** on screen. You lose a life instantly upon contact with any enemy or projectile. The game ends when the counter goes from **"x0"** to **"x-1"**.

## Gameplay Overview

A **timer bar** counts down at the top of the screen. Waves of enemies continuously spawn from the edges of the screen. A level is completed when the timer reaches zero **and** all remaining enemies have been defeated.

## Power-ups

Power-ups are dropped by defeated enemies. Pick them up to gain temporary advantages or permanent benefits.

| Power-up | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **1 Coin** | Adds 1 coin to your total |
| **5 Coin** | Adds 5 coins to your total |
| **1-Up** | Grants an extra life |
| **Coffee** | Increases movement speed for approximately 16 seconds |
| **Heavy Machine Gun** | Increases fire rate for approximately 12 seconds |
| **Screen Nuke** | Destroys all enemies on screen (no drops from nuked enemies) |
| **Shotgun** | Fires three bullets in a cone, but at a slower rate for approximately 12 seconds |
| **Smoke Bomb** | Teleports the player; enemies freeze with question marks above their heads for approximately 4 seconds |
| **Sheriff Badge** | Combines the effects of the machine gun, shotgun, and coffee with slightly lower individual bonuses; lasts approximately 24 seconds |
| **Tombstone** | Activates zombie mode — you kill enemies on contact, and enemies flee from you for approximately 8 seconds. **Does NOT protect you from boss bullets** |
| **Wagon Wheel** | Fire in all 8 directions simultaneously for approximately 12 seconds |

## Upgrades

After completing certain levels, a **Vendor** appears and offers upgrades in exchange for coins. The Vendor appears after levels **1-2**, **1-4**, **2-1**, **2-3**, **3-1**, and **3-3**.

### Slot 1 — Boots
- **8 Coins** (first purchase)
- **20 Coins** (second purchase)
- Upgrades to **1-Up** for **10 Coins**

### Slot 2 — Gun
- **10 Coins** (first purchase)
- **20 Coins** (second purchase)
- **30 Coins** (third purchase)
- Upgrades to **Super-Gun** for **99 Coins** (Hard Mode only; requires all other gun and ammo upgrades)
- **Sheriff Badge** for **10 Coins**

### Slot 3 — Ammo
- **15 Coins** (first purchase)
- **30 Coins** (second purchase)
- **45 Coins** (third purchase)
- Upgrades to **Sheriff Badge** for **10 Coins**

## Enemies

### Stage 1 — Prairie

| Enemy | HP | Behavior |
|-------|-----|----------|
| **Orc** | 1 HP | Walks slowly; spawns in large numbers |
| **Spikeball** | 2 HP (moving) / 7 HP (deployed) | Deploys at a random location and kills on contact. If the timer runs out and only spikeballs remain, their HP is reduced to 1 |
| **Ogre** | 3 HP | Moves slowly; destroys spikeballs on contact |

### Stage 2 — Forest

| Enemy | HP | Behavior |
|-------|-----|----------|
| **Mushroom** | 2 HP | Moves quickly |
| **Evil Butterfly** | 1 HP | Flying enemy; can spawn anywhere on the screen perimeter |

### Stage 3 — Graveyard

| Enemy | HP | Behavior |
|-------|-----|----------|
| **Mummy** | 6 HP | Moves slowly; spawns in large hordes |
| **Imp** | 3 HP | Flying enemy; actively seeks the player. **Not distracted by Gopher or Tombstone** |

## Friendlies

### Gopher
- Has **infinite HP**
- Distracts enemies and draws them toward it
- Kills enemies on impact
- **Cannot be killed by the player**
- Disappears after crossing the screen
- May carry the player on a parade float after defeating a boss

## Bosses

### Cowboy
- **50 HP** in the first fight
- **100 HP** in the second fight
- Separated from the player by a ravine/river
- Shoots volleys upward, sometimes matching the player's movements
- Drops an **extra life** upon defeat
- A **bridge** appears after defeat, and a **raft** pickup sends you to the next stage
- Appears in **Stage 1-5** and **Stage 2-4**

### Fector
- **350 HP**
- The **final boss** of the game
- Fires directly at the player or in all **8 directions**
- Summons additional enemies as his health decreases
- Appears in **Stage 3-4**

## Levels

The game is divided into three stages, each with multiple areas:

| Stage | Theme | Areas |
|-------|-------|-------|
| **Stage 1** | Prairie | Areas 1-4 + Boss |
| **Stage 2** | Forest | Areas 1-3 + Boss (Area 4 is the boss) |
| **Stage 3** | Graveyard | Areas 1-3 + Boss (Area 4 is the boss) |

## Hard Mode

**Hard Mode** is unlocked after completing the game for the first time.

- Upgrades and coins **carry over**
- Lives **reset to x3**
- Held power-ups are **removed**
- Enemies spawn in **greater numbers**, move **faster**, and have **extra HP**
- **Imps** spawn in every level except boss fights
- **Extra lives** drop less frequently
- Can be beaten **multiple times**, with difficulty increasing each time

## Saving

The game **autosaves** after each completed level and after each death. Your held item, upgrades, lives, and remaining time are all saved, allowing you to resume later.

**Important:** The game **does NOT autosave if you die during a boss fight**. This can be exploited to retry bosses without losing lives.

## Achievements

| Achievement | Requirement |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Prairie King** | Complete Journey of the Prairie King |
| **Fector's Challenge** | Complete Journey of the Prairie King **without dying** (you can exploit the save system to help achieve this) |

## Tips and Strategies

1. **Prioritize movement** — Staying mobile is key to avoiding enemies and projectiles.
2. **Save coins for upgrades** — Gun and ammo upgrades provide permanent benefits that make later stages much easier.
3. **Use Screen Nuke wisely** — Save it for when you are overwhelmed by a large group of enemies.
4. **Exploit the save system for bosses** — Since the game does not autosave during boss fights, you can retry difficult bosses without losing lives.
5. **Watch out for Imps in Hard Mode** — They actively seek you out and are not distracted by Gophers or Tombstones.
6. **Tombstone does not protect from boss bullets** — Do not rely on it during boss fights.
7. **Diagonal movement and shooting** — Mastering diagonal controls gives you a significant advantage in maneuvering and attacking.

## Trivia

- Random elements in Journey of the Prairie King are **NOT affected by Luck**.
- There is a **Lost Book** about this game that can be found in Stardew Valley.
- The title likely references the **"River King"** games by Marvelous.
- Pressing **Esc** exits the game instead of pausing it. The **P** key also does not pause the game.
