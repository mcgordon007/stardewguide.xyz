# Stardew Valley House Upgrades Guide: Complete Progression

Your farmhouse is your home base in Stardew Valley, and upgrading it unlocks essential features like cooking, marriage, children, and artisan goods aging. This comprehensive guide covers the complete house upgrade progression, including costs, materials, and what each upgrade unlocks.

## Overview of House Upgrades

The farmhouse can be upgraded three times, each adding new rooms and features. All upgrades are purchased from Robin at the Carpenter's Shop and require gold and materials. Construction takes 3 days for each upgrade.

### Upgrade Progression

1. **Starter House**: Basic one-room farmhouse
2. **First Upgrade**: Adds kitchen and bedroom expansion
3. **Second Upgrade**: Adds nursery for children
4. **Third Upgrade**: Adds cellar for aging goods

---

## Starter House

### Description
Your starting farmhouse is a modest one-room building with basic amenities. It includes a single bed, a small living area, a fireplace, and a TV. The house is functional but limited in features.

### Features

**Layout**:
- Single room design
- One bed (single)
- Fireplace
- TV (weather channel)
- Small living space
- No kitchen

**Limitations**:
- Cannot cook
- Cannot marry (no double bed)
- Limited storage space
- No children possible
- No cellar access

### Starting Furniture

| Item | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Single Bed | Sleep |
| Fireplace | Decoration |
| TV | Weather forecast |
| Rug | Decoration |

## First Upgrade - Kitchen

### Cost and Materials

**Cost**: 10,000g
**Materials**: Wood (450)
**Construction Time**: 3 days

### Features Added

**Kitchen**:
- Full cooking functionality
- Stove for cooking recipes
- Refrigerator for ingredient storage
- Counter space

**Bedroom Expansion**:
- Double bed (replaces single bed)
- Larger bedroom area
- Marriage capability unlocked

**Living Room Expansion**:
- Expanded living space
- More room for furniture
- Better layout

### Unlocks

**Cooking System**:
- Access to all cooking recipes
- Cook meals for energy and buffs
- Gift cooked items to villagers
- Refrigerator stores ingredients

**Marriage**:
- Double bed allows spouse to move in
- Can propose to marriage candidates
- Spouse lives in farmhouse
- Spouse helps with farm tasks

### Kitchen Functionality

**Cooking Process**:
1. Approach stove
2. Select recipe from menu
3. Recipe shows required ingredients
4. Cook if ingredients available
5. Meal appears in inventory

**Refrigerator**:
- Stores up to 36 items
- Ingredients accessible while cooking
- Keeps food fresh
- Convenient storage

### Cooking Benefits

**Energy Restoration**:
| Food | Energy | Health |
|------|--------|--------|
| Fried Egg | 50 | 20 |
| Omelet | 100 | 50 |
| Hashbrowns | 80 | 30 |
| Complete Breakfast | 200 | 100 |

**Buff Foods**:
| Food | Buff | Duration |
|------|------|----------|
| Spicy Eel | +1 Speed, +1 Luck | 7m |
| Lucky Lunch | +3 Luck | 11m |
| Dish o' The Sea | +3 Fishing | 5m |
| Miner's Treat | +3 Mining | 5m |

## Second Upgrade - Bedroom/Nursery

### Cost and Materials

**Cost**: 65,000g
**Materials**: Hardwood (100)
**Construction Time**: 3 days

### Features Added

**Nursery**:
- Second bedroom for children
- Two cribs for two children
- Children's room decoration
- Separate from main bedroom

**Expanded Space**:
- Larger overall house
- More furniture placement options
- Additional storage space
- Better layout

### Unlocks

**Children**:
- Can have up to two children
- Children appear after marriage
- Children grow through stages
- Family life experience

### Children System

**Having Children**:
- Must be married for at least 7 days
- Spouse asks about having children
- Choose to have or decline
- Child appears 14 days after agreement

**Child Stages**:
| Stage | Duration | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Stage 1 (Baby) | 14 days | Sleeping in crib |
| Stage 2 | 14 days | Standing/walking |
| Stage 3 | 28 days | Crawling |
| Stage 4 (Toddler) | Permanent | Full toddler stage |

**Two Children**:
- Second child possible after first reaches toddler stage
- Same process as first child
- Maximum two children per family

### Children Interactions

**Baby Stage**:
- Can be held
- Makes baby sounds
- Stays in crib

**Toddler Stage**:
- Walks around house
- Can be picked up
- Plays with toys

**Child Stage**:
- Runs around house
- Can be talked to
- Has personality based on spouse

## Third Upgrade - Cellar

### Cost and Materials

**Cost**: 100,000g
**Materials**: None
**Construction Time**: 3 days

### Features Added

**Cellar**:
- Underground room accessed via staircase
- 33 Cask slots for aging
- Temperature-controlled environment
- Perfect for wine and cheese aging

**Casks**:
- 33 casks included
- Each cask ages one product
- Products improve quality over time
- Iridium quality achievable

### Unlocks

**Product Aging**:
- Wine can be aged to iridium quality
- Cheese can be aged to iridium quality
- Goat cheese can be aged
- Value increases significantly

### Cask Aging Mechanics

**Quality Progression**:
| Stage | Quality | Value Multiplier |
|-------|---------|------------------|
| Base | Normal | 1x |
| Silver | Silver | 1.25x |
| Gold | Gold | 1.5x |
| Iridium | Iridium | 2x |

**Aging Times**:
| Product | Time to Iridium |
|---------|-----------------|
| Wine | 2 seasons (56 days) |
| Cheese | ~14 days |
| Goat Cheese | ~14 days |
| Pale Ale | ~14 days |
| Beer | ~7 days |

### Wine Aging Value Analysis

- Worth aging for high-value wines

## Upgrade Strategy

### Priority Recommendations

**First Upgrade Priority**:
- Kitchen upgrade should be first priority
- Unlocks cooking for energy/buffs
- Enables marriage
- Essential for game progression

**Second Upgrade Priority**:
- Bedroom upgrade for family life
- Optional if you don't want children
- Consider after marriage
- Adds storage space

**Third Upgrade Priority**:
- Cellar upgrade for artisan goods
- Best after establishing wine production
- Requires casks to be useful
- Late-game investment

### Timing Recommendations

**Kitchen Upgrade**:
- Best time: First year, after earning 10,000g
- Priority: High
- Reason: Cooking and marriage essential

**Bedroom Upgrade**:
- Best time: After marriage, when wanting children
- Priority: Medium
- Reason: Optional for family gameplay

**Cellar Upgrade**:
- Best time: After establishing keg production
- Priority: Medium-High
- Reason: Maximizes wine profits

## Material Gathering

### Wood (450 for Kitchen)

**Sources**:
- Chop trees on farm
- Chop trees in forest
- Buy from Robin (not recommended)
- Clear farm debris

**Time Estimate**: 1-2 days of chopping

### Hardwood (100 for Bedroom)

**Sources**:
- Large stumps on Forest Farm
- Secret Woods (12 daily)
- Hardwood nodes in mines
- Buy from Robin occasionally

**Time Estimate**: 8-10 days for bedroom upgrade

## Tips for House Upgrades

### Planning Tips

1. **Save materials in advance**: Gather wood, stone, hardwood before purchasing
2. **Plan timing**: Upgrade during convenient seasons
3. **Consider order**: Kitchen first, then bedroom, then cellar
4. **Budget wisely**: Save gold progressively

### Construction Tips

1. **Check Robin's schedule**: Shop closed Mondays
2. **Have materials ready**: Cannot purchase without materials
3. **Plan around festivals**: Construction continues during festivals
4. **Prepare for changes**: House layout changes during construction

### Usage Tips

**Kitchen**:
- Cook daily for energy
- Store ingredients in refrigerator
- Learn recipes from TV and villagers
- Gift cooked foods

**Nursery**:
- Decide on children early
- Interact with children daily
- Enjoy family narrative

**Cellar**:
- Age high-value wines first
- Rotate products efficiently
- Plan aging schedule
- Use all 33 casks

House upgrades are essential milestones in Stardew Valley progression. The kitchen upgrade unlocks cooking and marriage, the bedroom upgrade enables family life, and the cellar upgrade maximizes artisan goods value.
