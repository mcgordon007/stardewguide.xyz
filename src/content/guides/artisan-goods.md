# Artisan Goods Guide

![Artisan Goods](/images/artisan-illustration.jpg)

Artisan goods are processed items created from raw crops, animal products, and other materials using specialized equipment. They are typically much more profitable than selling raw ingredients, making artisan processing one of the most important gold-making strategies in Stardew Valley. This guide covers all artisan equipment, processing times, input and output items, profit margins, the aging system for wine and cheese, and overall profit rankings.

## Keg

The Keg is one of the most profitable artisan machines in the game. It processes fruits into wine, vegetables into juice, and honey into mead. The crafting recipe is unlocked at Farming Level 8.

### Wine Production

### Mead Production

Honey can be processed into mead in a keg. Mead sells for 400g base (560g with Artisan). The processing time is approximately 10-12 days. Mead is a decent option if you have many bee houses, but it is less profitable than fruit wine.

## Aging System: Casks

Casks are special artisan equipment that can age certain products to increase their quality and sell price. Casks can only be placed inside the farmhouse cellar (unlocked after upgrading the house to the third level) or inside a Shed.

### Cask Crafting

Casks are crafted from 20 Wood and 1 Keg. They are unlocked at Farming Level 14 (via the Mastery system, which requires all 5 skills at Level 10).

## Tips for Maximizing Artisan Profits

1. **Get the Artisan profession** at Farming Level 10 (Tiller branch). The 40% bonus to all artisan goods is the single most impactful money multiplier in the game.
2. **Build processing facilities early.** Even 10-20 kegs or preserves jars dramatically increase income over time.
3. **Fill the Greenhouse with Ancient Fruit** for year-round wine production. This is the most profitable long-term strategy.
4. **Process everything.** Never sell raw crops or animal products if you can process them instead.
5. **Use the Tiller + Artisan combo** for maximum crop-based profits.
6. **Age wine and cheese in casks** for the highest possible sell prices.
7. **Automate with sprinklers.** Spend less time watering and more time processing.
8. **Place artisan equipment in sheds** for organized processing areas.
9. **Build kegs in bulk.** Each keg requires 30 wood, 1 copper bar, 1 iron bar, and 1 oak resin. Stockpile these materials.
10. **Use Deluxe Speed-Gro or Hyper Speed-Gro** on crops to reduce growth time and increase harvests per season.
