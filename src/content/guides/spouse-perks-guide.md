# Complete Guide to Spouse Perks and Marriage Benefits in Stardew Valley

Getting married in Stardew Valley is more than just a romantic milestone. Your spouse becomes an active participant in your daily farm life, providing gifts, meals, and even helping with chores. This guide covers every perk and benefit that comes with marriage, along with the mechanics of the Bouquet, Mermaid's Pendant, and the jealousy system.

## The Path to Marriage

### The Bouquet

The Bouquet is the first major milestone on the road to marriage. Here are the key details:

- **Cost**: 200g at Pierre's General Store.
- **Availability**: Purchasable after reaching 8 hearts with any marriage candidate.
- **Effect**: Giving a Bouquet to a marriage candidate at 8 hearts confirms your romantic interest. Their friendship meter changes to show hearts instead of the standard neutral display.
- **Important Note**: You can only give a Bouquet to one NPC at a time. If you give a Bouquet to someone else, the first recipient's bouquet status is removed (though their friendship level remains).

### The Mermaid's Pendant

The Mermaid's Pendant is the item that triggers the marriage proposal:

- **Cost**: 5,000g.
- **Where to Buy**: From the Old Mariner, who appears on the beach dock during rainy days.
- **Requirement**: You must have repaired the beach bridge (300 wood) and be at 10 hearts with the recipient.
- **Effect**: Giving the Mermaid's Pendant to your 10-heart partner triggers a marriage cutscene. The wedding takes place 3 days later.

### Wedding Day

Three days after giving the Mermaid's Pendant, your wedding ceremony takes place. All villagers attend the ceremony (except those who are sick or otherwise occupied). After the wedding, your spouse moves into your farmhouse.

## Daily Spouse Activities

### Morning Routine

Your spouse has a consistent morning routine after marriage:

- **Wake Up**: Your spouse wakes up at the same time as you (6:00 AM).
- **Breakfast Interaction**: Your spouse will stand near the kitchen area in the morning.
- **Standing Location**: Each spouse has a preferred spot in the farmhouse where they stand during different times of day.

### Daytime Activities

Spouses do not stay in the farmhouse all day. They follow a schedule that includes:

- **Leaving the House**: Most spouses leave the farmhouse at some point during the day to visit their original locations or other areas of Pelican Town.
- **Social Visits**: Spouses may visit other NPCs or locations that were important to them before marriage.
- **Return Home**: All spouses return to the farmhouse by the end of the day (usually by 6:00 PM or later).

## Spouse Gifts

### Breakfast Gift

One of the most useful spouse perks is the daily breakfast gift. Each morning, your spouse may give you a food item:

- **Frequency**: Your spouse gives you a gift on most mornings, but not every day.
- **Gift Type**: The gift is always a cooked dish or food item.
- **Quality**: Spouse gifts are always normal quality.
- **Energy Restoration**: These gifts can be eaten for energy and health restoration, saving you from using your own cooked food.

### Special Anniversary Gift

On your wedding anniversary each year, your spouse will give you a special gift. This is typically a unique or rare item:

- **Year 1**: A bouquet or flower arrangement.
- **Subsequent Years**: Various items including rare foods, flowers, or other gifts depending on the spouse.

### Gift-Giving to Your Spouse

You can continue giving gifts to your spouse after marriage:

- **Gift Limit**: One gift per day with no weekly limit. However, friendship gains from gifts are halved compared to regular villagers.
- **Birthday Bonus**: Giving a loved or liked gift on your spouse's birthday provides the standard 8x friendship bonus.
- **Stardrop**: If your spouse gives you a Stardrop at 12.5 hearts (reached by giving them a gift every day after max hearts), this is a permanent max energy increase.

## Spouse Farm Help

### Watering Crops

One of the most valuable spouse perks is farm help. Your spouse will occasionally water crops for you:

- **Chance**: Each morning, there is a chance your spouse will water some of your crops.
- **Coverage**: They typically water about 15-20 tiles of crops.
- **Limitation**: They do not water all crops, only a portion.
- **Priority**: They tend to water crops closest to the farmhouse first.

### Repairing Fences

Your spouse may also repair broken fences on your farm:

- **Chance**: Similar to crop watering, there is a daily chance of fence repair.
- **Coverage**: They repair a limited number of broken fence sections.
- **Fence Types**: They repair any type of fence, including hardwood fences.

### Feeding Animals

Some spouses will help feed your farm animals:

- **Chance**: A daily chance triggers this behavior.
- **Coverage**: They feed a portion of your animals, not necessarily all of them.
- **Hay Requirement**: They use hay from your silos, so make sure you have enough stored.

### What Spouses Do NOT Help With

It is important to note the limitations of spouse farm help:

- They do not harvest crops.
- They do not plant seeds.
- They do not collect animal products (milk, eggs, wool, etc.).
- They do not clear debris (rocks, trees, weeds).
- They do not refill pet bowls.

## The Jealousy Mechanic

### How Jealousy Works

The jealousy mechanic is an important consideration for married players who enjoy socializing with other NPCs:

- **Trigger**: Giving a gift to another marriage candidate of the same gender as your spouse, who has previously received a Bouquet from you, can trigger jealousy.
- **Effect**: Your spouse will confront you with a jealous dialogue the next day.
- **Friendship Penalty**: Jealousy causes a small friendship decrease with your spouse (approximately -30 friendship points).
- **Frequency**: Jealousy can trigger multiple times if you continue giving gifts to other candidates.

### How to Avoid Jealousy

To prevent jealousy from affecting your marriage:

- **Stop Giving Gifts to Other Candidates**: Once married, focus your daily gift on your spouse.
- **Give Gifts to Non-Candidates**: Giving gifts to NPCs who are not marriage candidates (like Gus, Clint, or the Wizard) does not trigger jealousy.
- **Different Gender Candidates**: Giving gifts to marriage candidates of a different gender than your spouse will not trigger jealousy.
- **Birthday Gifts**: Birthday gifts to same-gender marriage candidates who have received your Bouquet can trigger jealousy, so be cautious.

### Jealousy Dialogue

Each spouse has unique jealousy dialogue. Some examples include:

- **Abigail**: "I saw you giving a gift to someone else today... I thought I was special."
- **Sebastian**: "You were with someone else today, weren't you? I could tell."
- **Haley**: "I can't believe you gave a gift to someone else. That's so rude."

## The Stardrop from Your Spouse

### 12.5 Hearts Mechanic

After reaching maximum hearts (10 hearts) with your spouse, you can continue giving them one gift per day. After giving them a gift every day for a period of time, you will eventually reach 12.5 hearts (displayed as a glowing effect around the heart meter). At this point, your spouse will give you a **Stardrop** as a token of their love.

### Stardrop Effect

The Stardrop from your spouse permanently increases your maximum energy by 34 points, just like all other Stardrops. This is one of the seven Stardrops available in the game and is often the easiest one to obtain since it simply requires consistent daily gifting to your spouse.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can my spouse die?**
No, spouses in Stardew Valley cannot die or be permanently lost.

**What happens if my spouse's friendship drops below 10 hearts?**
Your spouse will not leave, but they will become unhappy. Their dialogue will change to reflect the strained relationship. If friendship drops to 0 hearts, they will still live in your house but will be very cold toward you.

**Can I have multiple spouses?**
No, you can only be married to one person at a time. You must divorce your current spouse before marrying someone else.

**Does the jealousy mechanic apply to Krobus as a roommate?**
No, the jealousy mechanic only applies to married spouses. Krobus as a roommate does not get jealous.

**How long does it take to reach 12.5 hearts?**
After reaching 10 hearts, you need to give your spouse a loved gift every day for approximately 7-14 days to reach 12.5 hearts and receive the Stardrop.
