# Advanced Iridium Rod Guide

The Advanced Iridium Rod is the ultimate fishing tool introduced in Stardew Valley 1.6.9, taking the already powerful Iridium Rod to the next level with an additional tackle slot. This rod represents the pinnacle of fishing equipment and is a must-have for serious anglers pursuing legendary fish and deep Skull Cavern lava pools.

## TL;DR

The Advanced Iridium Rod (added in 1.6.9) is the best fishing rod in the game. It features **two tackle slots** instead of one, allowing you to combine tackle effects like never before. Unlock it by achieving **Fishing Mastery** from the Mastery Cave (requires all 5 skills at level 10), where you receive your first rod for free. Additional rods can be purchased from Willy's Fish Shop for 25,000g. Combine tackle strategically (e.g., Cork Bobber + Curiosity Lure) to make even the most difficult catches manageable.

## The Two Tackle Slots

The Advanced Iridium Rod's defining feature is its **dual tackle slots**, allowing you to equip two different tackle items simultaneously. This creates powerful combinations that were previously impossible.

## Is the Advanced Iridium Rod Worth It?

### For Casual Players

If you only fish occasionally for Community Center bundles or supplemental income, the regular Iridium Rod is sufficient. The 25,000g investment may be better spent on other priorities.

### For Serious Anglers

The Advanced Iridium Rod is **absolutely worth it** if you:

- Are pursuing the five **Legendary Fish** (extremely high difficulty)
- Fish regularly for significant income
- Want to catch **Legendary Fish II** in the extended family quest
- Explore the **Volcano Dungeon's lava pools** for rare fish
- Participate in the **SquidFest** and **Trout Derby** events
- Are working toward the **Fishing Mastery** achievement

## Tips for Using the Advanced Iridium Rod

1. **Always use bait** - The rod supports bait; never fish without it. Wild Bait is best for double catches.

2. **Match tackle to your target**:
   - **Legendary fish**: Cork Bobber + Curiosity Lure
   - **Treasure farming**: Treasure Hunter + Magnet
   - **General fishing**: Cork Bobber + Sonar
   - **Rainy days**: Dress Spinner + Cork Bobber

3. **Food buffs stack with tackle** - Eat Dish o' The Sea (+1 Fishing) or Seafoam Pudding (+4 Fishing) before difficult catches.

4. **Save breakable tackle for important catches** - Don't waste Barbed Hook or Trap Bobber on common fish.

5. **Bring spare tackle** - Breakable tackle can fail mid-dungeon; carry backups for Skull Cavern or Volcano fishing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does the Advanced Iridium Rod make legendary fish easy?

It makes them **significantly easier** but not trivial. Legendary fish still have difficulty ratings of 80-110. The dual tackle slots give you more tools to manage their erratic movement, but skill and practice are still required.

### Can I use two of the same tackle?

Yes! You can equip two identical tackle items, and their effects **stack**. This works particularly well with Trap Bobber (double slowdown) and Cork Bobber (double bar size). The only exceptions are Sonar Bobber and Curiosity Lure, which are unique effects that cannot be duplicated.

### Does the Advanced Iridium Rod work with all bait types?

Yes, it supports all bait types: standard Bait, Wild Bait, and Magnet.

### Can I lose the Advanced Iridium Rod?

Like all tools, you cannot lose or break the Advanced Iridium Rod. If you somehow misplace it (e.g., by dying in the Mines), it will be in the Lost Items box at the Adventurer's Guild or can be recovered through the Lost Items merchant.

### Is there any rod better than Advanced Iridium?

As of version 1.6.15, the Advanced Iridium Rod is the best and final fishing rod in the game. No further upgrades exist.

### The Lucky Purple Shorts Secret

If you wear Lewis's **Lucky Purple Shorts** while fishing with the Advanced Iridium Rod, you get a hidden **+1 Fishing** buff. This is a secret easter egg from ConcernedApe!
