# Complete Guide to Mushroom Trees in Stardew Valley

Mushroom trees are a unique type of tree in Stardew Valley that produce valuable mushrooms instead of standard tree products. They come in several varieties, each with its own spawning conditions and products. This guide covers everything you need to know about mushroom tree spawning, types, harvesting methods, and processing.

## Overview of Mushroom Trees

### What Are Mushroom Trees?

Mushroom trees (also called mushroom logs or mushroom stumps) are a special type of tree that grows on the player's farm and in certain wild areas. Unlike regular trees that produce wood, sap, and syrup, mushroom trees produce various types of mushrooms that can be foraged or tapped.

### Mushroom Tree Types

There are four main types of mushroom trees in Stardew Valley:

| Type | Color | Mushroom Produced | Spawn Location |
|------|-------|-------------------|---------------|
| Common Mushroom Tree | Brown/Tan | Common Mushroom | Farm, Secret Woods |
| Red Mushroom Tree | Red | Red Mushroom | Farm, Secret Woods |
| Purple Mushroom Tree | Purple | Purple Mushroom | Farm (rare), Underground |
| Magma Cap | Orange/Red | Magma Cap | Ginger Island (volcano area) |

## Mushroom Tree Spawning

### How Mushroom Trees Spawn

Mushroom trees spawn under specific conditions:

1. **Random Spawning**: Mushroom trees have a small chance of spawning each night on the farm, similar to regular trees. The spawn chance is affected by the number of open tiles and nearby trees.
2. **Mushroom Tree Seed**: You can plant Mushroom Tree Seeds (obtained from the Mushroom Cave or by foraging) to grow specific mushroom trees.
3. **Existing Stumps**: When a mushroom tree is chopped down, it leaves a stump. The stump can regrow into a new mushroom tree over time.

### Spawn Conditions

| Condition | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Season | Any season (mushroom trees are not seasonal) |
| Location | Farm, Secret Woods, Ginger Island |
| Tile Requirement | Must be on tillable or non-tillable dirt/grass |
| Space Needed | 1 tile for the tree, surrounding tiles must be clear |
| Spawn Rate | Low (approximately 0.5-1% per eligible tile per night) |

### Farm Cave Mushrooms

If you chose the mushroom cave option for your farm cave (instead of fruit bats), the cave will produce mushrooms daily:

- **Common Mushroom**: Available from the start.
- **Red Mushroom**: Unlocks after 25 mushrooms harvested.
- **Purple Mushroom**: Unlocks after 50 mushrooms harvested.
- **Magma Cap**: Unlocks after 100 mushrooms harvested (after Ginger Island access).

## Mushroom Tree Types in Detail

### Common Mushroom Tree

**Appearance**: Brown/tan colored mushroom cap on a short, thick trunk.

**Products**:
- **Foraged**: Common Mushroom (edible, restores 25 energy)
- **Tapped**: Common Mushroom (via Tapper, produces every 2 days)
- **Sell Price**: Common Mushroom sells for 30g (base), 37g (silver), 45g (gold), 60g (iridium)

**Processing**:
- Can be used in cooking recipes (Mushroom Rice, etc.)
- Can be placed in a Preserves Jar to make Pickled Mushroom
- Can be used in the Seed Maker

### Red Mushroom Tree

**Appearance**: Bright red mushroom cap with white spots, similar to the classic Amanita muscaria.

**Products**:
- **Foraged**: Red Mushroom (edible, restores 38 energy)
- **Tapped**: Red Mushroom (via Tapper, produces every 2 days)
- **Sell Price**: Red Mushroom sells for 75g (base), 93g (silver), 112g (gold), 150g (iridium)

**Processing**:
- Used in cooking recipes
- Can be pickled in a Preserves Jar
- Higher value than Common Mushrooms

### Purple Mushroom Tree

**Appearance**: Deep purple mushroom cap on a dark trunk. These are the rarest mushroom trees on the surface.

**Products**:
- **Foraged**: Purple Mushroom (edible, restores 50 energy)
- **Tapped**: Purple Mushroom (via Tapper, produces every 2 days)
- **Sell Price**: Purple Mushroom sells for 150g (base), 187g (silver), 225g (gold), 300g (iridium)

**Processing**:
- Used in cooking recipes
- Can be pickled in a Preserves Jar
- High-value mushroom, worth preserving

### Magma Cap (Ginger Island)

**Appearance**: Orange-red mushroom with a cracked, volcanic appearance. Found exclusively on Ginger Island.

**Products**:
- **Foraged**: Magma Cap (edible, restores 75 energy)
- **Tapped**: Magma Cap (via Tapper, produces every 2 days)
- **Sell Price**: Magma Cap sells for 200g (base), 250g (silver), 300g (gold), 400g (iridium)

**Processing**:
- Used in cooking recipes
- Can be pickled in a Preserves Jar
- Exclusive to Ginger Island volcano area

## Foraging vs. Tree Tapping

### Foraging Mushrooms

**How It Works**:
- Mushroom trees that are fully grown will occasionally produce a mushroom at their base.
- Walk up to the tree and click the mushroom to forage it.
- The mushroom regrows after a few days.

**Pros**:
- No equipment needed
- Immediate harvest
- Can forage from multiple trees per day

**Cons**:
- Less predictable than tapping
- Lower yield over time
- Requires checking trees daily

### Tree Tapping with Tappers

**How It Works**:
- Place a Tapper on a fully grown mushroom tree.
- The Tapper will produce a mushroom every 2 days.
- Collect the mushroom from the Tapper.

**Pros**:
- Consistent production every 2 days
- Hands-off once placed
- Can process many trees simultaneously

**Cons**:
- Requires Tappers (crafted from 40 Wood, 2 Copper Bar at Foraging Level 4)
- Takes up the Tapper slot on the tree
- Initial investment in Tapper materials

### Comparison Table

| Method | Yield | Frequency | Equipment | Best For |
|--------|-------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Foraging | 1 mushroom | Every few days | None | Early game, few trees |
| Tapping | 1 mushroom | Every 2 days | Tapper | Mid-late game, many trees |

### Recommendation

For maximum mushroom production, use Tappers on all your mushroom trees. This provides consistent, predictable yields and requires minimal daily effort. Place Tappers on every mushroom tree on your farm and collect mushrooms every 2 days.

## Mushroom Tree Seeds

### What Are Mushroom Tree Seeds?

Mushroom Tree Seeds are items that can be planted in tilled soil to grow mushroom trees. They are a reliable way to cultivate specific types of mushroom trees.

### How to Obtain Mushroom Tree Seeds

| Source | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Farm Cave (Mushroom option) | Harvesting mushrooms has a chance to drop seeds |
| Foraging | Digging up mushroom tree stumps may yield seeds |
| Traveling Cart | Occasionally sold for varying prices |
| Mushroom Tree Drop | Chopping down mushroom trees may drop seeds |

### Planting Mushroom Tree Seeds

1. **Till the Soil**: Use your hoe to till the tile where you want to plant the seed.
2. **Plant the Seed**: Select the Mushroom Tree Seed from your inventory and plant it.
3. **Wait for Growth**: The seed will grow through several stages before becoming a full mushroom tree.
4. **Growth Time**: Mushroom trees take approximately 10-15 days to fully mature from seed.

### Growth Stages

| Stage | Duration | Appearance |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Seed | Day 1 | Small dot on tilled soil |
| Sprout | Days 2-4 | Small green shoot |
| Sapling | Days 5-8 | Small mushroom shape |
| Young Tree | Days 9-12 | Medium mushroom tree |
| Mature Tree | Day 13+ | Full-sized mushroom tree |

## Tips and Strategies

### Farm Layout for Mushroom Trees

- **Dedicated Area**: Set aside a section of your farm specifically for mushroom trees.
- **Tapper Placement**: Place Tappers on every mature mushroom tree for maximum production.
- **Spacing**: Leave enough space between trees for easy navigation and harvesting.
- **Protection**: Mushroom trees are not affected by crows, so no scarecrows are needed.

### Maximizing Production

- **Farm Cave**: Choose the mushroom cave option for consistent daily mushroom production.
- **Multiple Trees**: Grow as many mushroom trees as space allows.
- **Tapper Management**: Check Tappers every 2 days for consistent harvesting.
- **Processing**: Process mushrooms into higher-value products for maximum profit.

### Seasonal Considerations

- **All Seasons**: Mushroom trees produce in all seasons, unlike regular crops.
- **Winter Advantage**: Mushroom production continues in winter when most crops cannot grow.
- **Consistent Income**: Mushroom trees provide a reliable income source year-round.

Creating a dedicated area for mushroom trees maximizes production:

### Indoor Mushroom Production

While mushroom trees must be grown outdoors, you can process mushrooms indoors:

- **Preserves Jar**: Place Common, Red, Purple, or Magma Caps in Preserves Jars for pickled mushrooms.
- **Drying Rack**: Dry mushrooms for increased value and energy restoration.
- **Cooking**: Use mushrooms in various cooking recipes.
- **Seed Maker**: Process mushrooms in Seed Makers for a chance at Mushroom Tree Seeds.

## Mushroom Cave vs. Mushroom Trees

### Comparison

| Factor | Mushroom Cave | Mushroom Trees |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| Setup Cost | Free (choice at start) | Mushroom Tree Seeds |
| Daily Production | 1-6 mushrooms/day | Varies by tree count |
| Mushroom Types | All 4 types (unlock progressively) | Depends on tree type |
| Tapper Compatible | No | Yes |
| Space Requirement | Fixed cave area | Flexible (outdoor farm) |
| Winter Production | Yes | Yes |
| Automation | Limited | High (with Tappers) |

### Which Is Better?

For most players, the **mushroom cave** is the better choice because:

1. **Consistent Production**: The cave produces mushrooms daily without any maintenance.
2. **Variety**: All four mushroom types become available over time.
3. **No Space Cost**: The cave does not take up valuable farm space.
4. **Early Game Value**: Mushroom cave production starts immediately.

However, **mushroom trees** are valuable as a supplement:

1. **Additional Production**: Trees provide mushrooms beyond what the cave produces.
2. **Tapper Synergy**: Trees can be tapped for consistent production.
3. **Tree Spread**: Mature trees can spawn new trees, expanding production over time.

**Can mushroom trees be chopped down for wood?**
Yes, you can chop down mushroom trees with an axe, but they yield fewer wood pieces than regular trees. Chopping them down also destroys the mushroom production.

**Do mushroom trees spread on their own?**
Yes, mushroom trees can spawn new mushroom trees nearby, similar to how regular trees spread. The spawn rate is low but consistent over time.

**Can I move a mushroom tree?**
No, mushroom trees cannot be moved once planted. You would need to chop them down and replant from seed.

**What happens if I choose the fruit bat cave instead of mushrooms?**
You can still grow mushroom trees on your farm regardless of your cave choice. The cave choice only affects what spawns inside the cave.

**Are Magma Caps available on the main farm?**
No, Magma Caps are exclusive to Ginger Island. They cannot be grown on the main farm.
