# Meadowlands Farm Guide

The Meadowlands Farm is the newest farm type introduced in Stardew Valley 1.6, designed specifically for players who want to focus on animal husbandry and ranching from the very beginning. Unlike other farms that require significant setup before animals become viable, Meadowlands gives you a head start with a functional coop and livestock right out of the gate.

## TL;DR

The Meadowlands Farm starts with a pre-built Chicken Coop and two chickens, plus a large pasture of Blue Grass that grows faster and provides better animal feed. The layout is optimized for ranching with ample grazing space, but offers less tillable land for crop-focused players. Best for: animal lovers, ranching-focused players, and beginners who want an easier early-game experience.

## Starting Advantages

### Pre-Built Chicken Coop

The Meadowlands Farm is the only farm type that begins with a **fully constructed Chicken Coop**. This provides immediate benefits:

- **Two chickens** are already living in the coop on Day 1
- **Eggs** can be collected starting from the first morning
- No need to save 4,000g and 300 wood for coop construction
- **Hay** is not initially stocked — instead of receiving 15 Parsnip Seeds from Mayor Lewis, you receive 15 Hay as your starting farm package

### Blue Grass Pasture

A significant portion of the Meadowlands Farm is covered in **Blue Grass**, a special type of grass introduced in 1.6:

| Property | Blue Grass | Regular Grass |
|----------|-----------|---------------|
| **Growth Speed** | Faster | Normal |
| **Animal Happiness** | Higher when eating | Normal |
| **Spread Rate** | Faster | Normal |
| **Appearance** | Blue-green tint | Standard green |

Blue Grass provides better nutrition for grazing animals, contributing to higher friendship growth and better quality products.

### Large Grazing Area

The farm layout features a wide, open central area perfect for animal grazing:

- Ample space for multiple barns and coops
- Natural fencing from terrain features
- Easy access to water sources for animals

## Farm Layout

### Tillable Area

The Meadowlands Farm has approximately **2,066 tillable tiles**, placing it on the smaller side compared to the Standard Farm's 3,427 tiles. The tillable land is concentrated in the northern and eastern portions of the farm.

### Non-Tillable Features

| Feature | Location | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| **Pond** | Southwest corner | Provides water for refilling the watering can |
| **Blue Grass Pasture** | Central area | Cannot be tilled; optimal for animal grazing |
| **Tree Groves** | Western edge | Source of wood and forage |
| **Cliff Walls** | Southern border | Natural boundary |

### Building Placement Strategy

For optimal Meadowlands Farm layout:

1. **Place barns and coops in the central pasture area** - close to the Blue Grass
2. **Reserve the northern tillable area for crops** - this is your main farming space
3. **Keep the western tree groves intact early** - you'll need wood for building more coops/barns
4. **Place silos near animal buildings** - convenient for hay storage and feeding

## Best Strategies

### Early Game (Spring Year 1)

1. **Collect eggs daily** from your starting chickens for immediate income
2. **Plant a small crop field** in the northern tillable area (parsnips, then potatoes)
3. **Let chickens graze** on Blue Grass rather than feeding hay when weather permits
4. **Build a Silo** as soon as possible (100g + 100 stone + 10 clay + 5 copper bars)
5. **Save for a Barn** by Summer to start raising cows for milk and cheese

### Mid Game (Summer - Fall Year 1)

1. **Expand your coop** to hold 8 chickens for consistent egg income
2. **Build a Barn** and purchase 2-4 cows
3. **Start making Artisan Goods** (Cheese, Mayonnaise) for higher profits
4. **Plant seasonal crops** in your limited tillable space for supplementary income
5. **Build a Mill** to turn wheat into flour for cooking

### Late Game (Year 2+)

1. **Max out coop and barn capacity** with Deluxe versions
2. **Add a Slime Hutch** or Fish Pond for diversified income
3. **Upgrade to Deluxe Barn/Coop** for auto-feeding systems
4. **Purchase an Auto-Petter** from Qi's Walnut Room to maintain animal friendship effortlessly
5. **Build a Shed** near your animal buildings for processing artisan goods

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- **Immediate animal income** from Day 1
- **No early-game coop investment** required (saves 4,000g + materials)
- **Blue Grass** provides superior animal nutrition
- **Beginner-friendly** - less pressure to balance crops and animals
- **Natural ranching aesthetic** - the farm looks great with animals roaming

### Cons

- **Less tillable land** than most farm types
- **Blue Grass cannot be removed** for additional farming space
- **Harder to focus on pure crop profits** (Ancient Fruit, Starfruit wine)
- **Late-game crop scaling** is more limited
- **Not ideal for Junimo Hut automation** due to irregular tillable areas

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I remove the Blue Grass to plant crops?

No, the central Blue Grass pasture is a permanent feature of the Meadowlands Farm and cannot be tilled or removed. This is the trade-off for the farm's animal-focused benefits.

### Do the starting chickens have names?

The two starting chickens are randomly assigned names and colors (typically white). You cannot choose their names when starting the game.

### Is Meadowlands Farm good for beginners?

Yes! The pre-built coop and immediate income from eggs make the early game much more forgiving. Beginners can learn animal care mechanics without the pressure of saving for buildings.

### Can I still do crop-focused play on Meadowlands?

Absolutely, but you'll need to be more strategic with your ~2,066 tillable tiles. Focus on high-value crops and use the Greenhouse for year-round production to maximize limited space.

### Does Blue Grass spread to other areas?

Yes, Blue Grass can spread to adjacent regular grass tiles, slowly converting your farm's grass areas. This is beneficial for animal grazing but cannot spread onto tilled soil.
