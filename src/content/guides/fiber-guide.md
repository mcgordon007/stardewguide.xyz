# Stardew Valley Fiber Guide

## Overview

**Fiber** is one of the most basic and essential resources in Stardew Valley. It is used in a wide variety of crafting recipes and is easily obtainable early in the game.

## How to Obtain Fiber

### Primary Sources
- **Cutting Weeds**: The most common source. Weeds appear on your farm and in the wild throughout all seasons.
- **Cutting Grass**: Using a Scythe on grass tufts can yield Fiber.
- **The Mines**: Weeds on mine levels can drop Fiber.
- **Mutant Bug Lair**: Located in the Sewers, this area is filled with weeds that drop Fiber.

### Other Sources
- **Recycling Machine**: Putting a Newspaper into a Recycling Machine has a chance to yield 3 Fiber.
- **Worm Bins**: Produce Fiber along with Bait.
- **Fiber Seeds**: Craftable after completing the Community Center or Joja Warehouse, these seeds grow into plants that yield Fiber.

## Farming Fiber

### Fiber Seeds Strategy
After unlocking Fiber Seeds (post-Community Center or Joja), you can farm Fiber efficiently:
- Fiber Seeds grow in all seasons, including Winter.
- They do not require watering.
- Each plant yields 4-7 Fiber when harvested.
- This is the most reliable way to mass-produce Fiber.

### Mutant Bug Lair
- Located in the Sewers (unlocked after donating 60 items to the Museum).
- Weeds here respawn daily, making it a great daily Fiber source.

## Tips

- Always keep a stockpile of Fiber for crafting needs.
- Fiber is needed in large quantities for Junimo Huts and Warp Totems.
- Don't sell Fiber directly; its sell price (1g) is negligible compared to its crafting value.
