# Stardew Valley Slime Hutch Guide

The Slime Hutch is a farm building that allows players to raise Slimes on their farm. It produces valuable Slime Balls that can be harvested for Slime and other items.

## Building Requirements

- **Cost:** 10,000g
- **Materials:**
  - 500 Stone
  - 10 Refined Quartz
  - 1 Iridium Bar
- **Builder:** Robin at the Carpenter's Shop
- **Build Time:** 3 days
- **Size:** 7x4 tiles (interior: 15x8 walkable tiles)

## Slime Hutch Basics

### Capacity
- The Slime Hutch can house up to **20 Slimes** at a time
- Slimes will breed and multiply if there are both male and female slimes present
- Slime gender is random and cannot be determined visually

### Water Troughs
- The hutch contains 4 water troughs that must be filled daily
- Use a watering can or rain totem to fill them
- If water troughs are empty, slimes will not produce Slime Balls
- Slimes will also attempt to escape if water troughs are empty

### Slime Behavior
- Slimes in the hutch are hostile and will attack the player on sight
- Wearing the **Slime Charmer Ring** prevents all damage from farm slimes
- Slimes cannot leave the hutch (they are contained within the building)
- Different colored slimes can be housed together

## Slime Ball Production

### How Slime Balls Spawn
- Slime Balls spawn randomly on the floor of the hutch
- They only spawn if all water troughs are filled
- More slimes = higher chance of Slime Ball production
- Up to 4 Slime Balls can spawn per day

### Slime Ball Contents
When broken, each Slime Ball yields:
- **10-20 Slime** (guaranteed)
- Small chance for additional items:
  - Petrified Slime
  - Emerald
  - Diamond
  - Ruby

### Harvesting Tips
- Enter the hutch wearing a Slime Charmer Ring to avoid damage
- Slime Balls can be broken with any tool or weapon
- Harvest Slime Balls daily for maximum production
- Slime sells for 5g each (base price)

## Slime Types and Colors

Slimes come in various colors depending on their origin:
- **Green Slimes:** Standard mine slimes
- **Blue Slimes:** Frozen levels of the mines
- **Red Slimes:** Lava levels of the mines
- **Purple Slimes:** Skull Cavern (drop Iridium Ore)
- **Tiger Slimes:** Ginger Island (drop Tiger Slime Eggs)
- **Yellow Slimes:** Special variants
- **Black Slimes:** Rare variants

## Slime Egg Production

- Slimes occasionally produce **Slime Eggs** instead of Slime Balls
- Slime Eggs can be:
  - Sold for profit (Green: 1,000g, Blue: 1,750g, Red: 2,500g, Purple: 5,000g)
  - Placed in a Slime Incubator to hatch new slimes
  - Placed in a Slime Press to produce 10-15 Slime

### Slime Incubator
- Crafted at Combat Level 8
- Materials: 2 Iridium Bars, 100 Slime
- Hatches Slime Eggs into slimes
- Placed inside the Slime Hutch

### Slime Press
- Crafted at Combat Level 6
- Materials: 25 Coal, 1 Fire Quartz, 1 Battery Pack
- Converts Slime Eggs into 10-15 Slime
- Generally less profitable than selling the eggs directly

## Profitability

### Income Sources
1. **Slime Balls:** 10-20 Slime per ball (5g each = 50-100g per ball)
2. **Slime Eggs:** 1,000g - 5,000g depending on color
3. **Gems from Slime Balls:** Occasional bonus drops

### Strategy Tips
- Focus on breeding Purple Slimes for the most valuable eggs
- Keep water troughs filled at all times
- Wear a Slime Charmer Ring when entering
- Consider the Slime Hutch as a passive income source rather than primary
- The Statue Of Endless Fortune can also produce Purple Slime Eggs

## Dangers and Safety

- Farm slimes deal damage like wild slimes
- Always wear a Slime Charmer Ring or bring healing items
- Keep the hutch door closed to prevent accidental entry
- Slimes cannot escape the building, so they pose no threat to farm animals

## Related Buildings and Items

- **Slime Incubator:** Hatches Slime Eggs
- **Slime Press:** Converts eggs to Slime
- **Slime Charmer Ring:** Prevents slime damage (Monster Eradication reward)
- **Statue Of Endless Fortune:** Can produce Purple Slime Eggs
