# Summer Crops Guide

![Summer Banner](/images/summer-banner.jpg)

Summer is the season with the highest raw profit potential in Stardew Valley. Starfruit wine is the single most profitable artisan good in the game, blueberries provide excellent multi-harvest returns, and hops enable near-daily Pale Ale production. This guide covers every summer crop in extreme detail, including seed sources, growth stages, selling prices at all quality levels, processing values, fertilizer strategies, and optimal planting plans.

## Starfruit

Starfruit is the most profitable crop in Stardew Valley when processed into wine. Each Starfruit Wine sells for 2,250g base, or 3,150g with the Artisan profession. However, seeds cost 400g each and must be purchased from the Oasis in the Calico Desert, which requires repairing the bus stop first.

| Normal Sell | 750g |
| Silver Sell | 937g |
| Gold Sell | 1,125g |
| Iridium Sell | 1,500g |
| Growth Time | 13 days |
| Gold per Day | 26.92g (single harvest) |
| Farming XP | 38 per harvest |
| Wine Value | 2,250g (3,150g with Artisan) |

### Growth Stages

| Small | Day 3-5 | Developing leaves |
| Medium | Day 6-8 | Larger star-shaped leaves |
| Large | Day 9-11 | Nearly mature, fruit forming |
| Harvestable | Day 13 | Golden starfruit visible |

### Best Uses

- Process into Starfruit Wine in kegs (2,250g base, 3,150g with Artisan) -- the most profitable artisan good in the game
- Sell raw at iridium quality for 1,500g
- Gift to villagers (several villagers like or love starfruit)
- Save a gold-quality starfruit for the Community Center Quality Crops Bundle

### Tips

Plant on Day 1 for a harvest on Day 14, then replant immediately for a second harvest on Day 27. With Deluxe Speed-Gro, the first harvest comes on Day 11 and the second on Day 24, leaving 4 days for a potential third planting that matures on Day 28. To maximize profit, pair starfruit with kegs -- a single plot with 2 harvests produces two wines worth 4,500g (6,300g with Artisan), for a net profit of 3,700g (5,500g with Artisan). For year-round production, use the Greenhouse and Ginger Island farm.

Melon is the best single-harvest summer crop available from Pierre's General Store. At 250g base sell price and only 80g per seed, melons offer an excellent return on investment. Melon Wine is also highly profitable, making melons a strong choice for players who cannot yet afford Starfruit seeds.

Melon Wine sells for 750g per keg -- if you have 50 kegs, two harvests of melons produce 100 wines worth 75,000g (105,000g with Artisan). This makes melons a very strong early-to-mid game winemaking crop before you can afford Starfruit seeds.

## Blueberry

Blueberries are the best multi-harvest summer crop. Each harvest produces 3 berries, and the plant regrows every 4 days. Planted on Day 1, a single blueberry plant yields 12 berries over 4 harvests by the end of summer, generating excellent total profit from a single seed purchase.

If you process all 12 into wine, that is 1,800g (2,520g with Artisan) from a single 80g seed investment. Plant on Day 1 and use Deluxe Fertilizer for a chance at iridium-quality berries. Note that quality does NOT affect wine or jelly price, so save fertilizer money if you plan to process everything.

## Hops

Hops are unique among summer crops because they regrow every single day after the initial 11-day growth period. This makes them the best crop for Pale Ale production in kegs. While the raw sell price is low (25g), Hops are not meant to be sold raw -- their value comes entirely from processing.

### Hops Pale Ale Profit Analysis

| Scenario | Harvests | Pale Ale Revenue | Seed Cost | Net Profit | Profit/Day |
|----------|----------|------------------|-----------|------------|------------|
| Raw sell | 18 | 450g | 60g | 390g | 13.93g |
| Pale Ale (no Artisan) | 18 | 5,400g | 60g | 5,340g | 190.71g |
| Pale Ale (Artisan) | 18 | 7,560g | 60g | 7,500g | 267.86g |

### Best Uses

- Process into Pale Ale in kegs (300g base, 420g with Artisan) -- this is where all the value is
- Never sell raw; always process
- One of the best crops for automated keg setups

### Tips

The key to hops profitability is having enough kegs. Since hops produce daily after maturity, you need one keg per hops plant to keep up with production. Each hop takes roughly 1.5-2 days to brew into Pale Ale, so having 2 kegs per hops plant ensures you never fall behind. With 18 harvests and the Artisan profession, a single hops plant generates 7,500g in Pale Ale profit over the season. If you plant 100 hops with 200 kegs, that is 750,000g per summer. This makes hops one of the most profitable crops in the game when you have the infrastructure.

If you plan to mine the Skull Cavern, growing hot peppers ensures a steady supply of Spicy Eel ingredients.

Corn is a cross-season crop that grows in both summer and fall. It has a long 14-day initial growth time but regrows every 4 days, allowing it to continue producing into fall. While not highly profitable on a per-harvest basis, its cross-season nature makes it useful for players who want continuous production without replanting.

Corn's main advantage is its cross-season longevity. However, the profit per day is still low compared to other summer crops. Corn is best used as a supplementary crop or for players who want to minimize replanting effort between seasons.

Once you have 3-5 plants, they will drop extra beans upon harvest, allowing you to expand exponentially. On Ginger Island's farm, coffee grows year-round, making it an excellent permanent source of both Coffee (for drinking and selling) and speed buffs.

With a 4-day growth cycle, wheat can be harvested 7 times from a Day 1 planting (days 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 28). Raw selling yields only 175g from 70g in seeds (105g profit), but brewing all 7 harvests into Beer produces 1,400g (1,960g with Artisan), netting 1,330g (1,890g with Artisan) profit. If you have many kegs and need a low-maintenance crop, wheat is a solid choice. It is also the cheapest way to gain farming XP in the early game.

## Fertilizer Comparison

Choosing the right fertilizer is critical for maximizing summer crop profits. There are two categories: speed fertilizers (reduce growth time) and quality fertilizers (increase crop quality chance).

### Speed Fertilizers

| Fertilizer | Growth Bonus | Best For | Cost / Acquisition |
|------------|-------------|----------|-------------------|
| Speed-Gro | 10% faster | Tight replanting schedules | Pierre: 100g (Year 2+) or craft: Oak Resin + Coral |
| Deluxe Speed-Gro | 25% faster | Fitting extra harvests | Pierre: 150g (Year 2+) or Oasis: 80g or craft: Pine Tar + Sodium Petrified Slime |
| Hyper Speed-Gro | 33% faster | Maximum harvest squeeze | Qi's Walnut Room: 80 Qi Gems or "A Master's Craft" quest reward |

Speed fertilizers work by reducing the total growth days using a floor function. For example, a 13-day Starfruit with Deluxe Speed-Gro (25% reduction) becomes floor(13 * 0.75) = floor(9.75) = 9 days. This means Starfruit planted on Day 1 with Deluxe Speed-Gro harvests on Day 10 instead of Day 14, allowing 3 harvests instead of 2 (days 10, 20, and 28 with replanting).

## Best Summer Strategies

### Strategy 1: Starfruit Winemaking (Most Profitable)

The single most profitable strategy in Stardew Valley. Requires bus repair (400g in Community Center bundles or Joja development) and enough capital for seeds.

1. Repair the bus by completing the Vault bundles or paying Joja (40,000g).
2. On Summer Day 1, travel to the Oasis and buy as many Starfruit seeds as possible (400g each).
3. Plant all Starfruit and water daily (or use sprinklers).
4. Harvest on Summer Day 14 and place in kegs immediately.
5. Replant and harvest again on Summer Day 27.
6. Repeat in the Greenhouse and on Ginger Island for year-round production.
7. With the Artisan profession, each Starfruit Wine sells for 3,150g.

**Expected profit per plot (2 harvests, Artisan): 5,500g**

### Strategy 2: Hops Pale Ale Factory (Best Sustained Income)

Requires a large number of kegs but produces incredible daily income. Hops regrow every day after the initial 11-day growth period.

1. Plant as many Hops as possible on Day 1 (aim for 100+ plants).
2. Set up 2 kegs per hops plant (200+ kegs for 100 plants).
3. After Day 11, harvest hops daily and immediately place in kegs.
4. Each Pale Ale sells for 300g (420g with Artisan).
5. 100 hops plants produce 18 Pale Ales each per season = 1,800 Pale Ales.
6. With Artisan: 1,800 x 420g = 756,000g per summer.

**Expected profit (100 plants, Artisan): 750,000g+**

### Strategy 3: Blueberry Multi-Harvest (Balanced Approach)

A great middle-ground strategy that requires less infrastructure than Hops but still produces strong returns.

1. Plant Blueberries on Day 1 with Deluxe Speed-Gro for a potential 5th harvest.
2. Harvest every 4 days starting Day 13.
3. Process berries into Wine or Jelly in kegs/preserves jars.
4. With 4 harvests of 3 berries each, a single plant yields 12 berries.
5. 12 wines at 210g each (Artisan) = 2,520g from an 80g seed.

**Expected profit per plot (4 harvests, Artisan wine): 2,440g**

### Strategy 4: Mixed Planting (Year 1 Friendly)

For players who cannot afford large-scale Starfruit planting or lack keg infrastructure.

- Dedicate 30% of farm to Melons (solid profit, 2 harvests)
- Dedicate 30% to Blueberries (multi-harvest, reliable)
- Dedicate 20% to Hot Peppers (fast income, Spicy Eel ingredients)
- Dedicate 10% to Hops (start building Pale Ale production)
- Dedicate 10% to Tomatoes or Wheat (cooking/beer ingredients)

### Strategy 5: Coffee Empire (Late Game)

Once you have Ginger Island unlocked and a steady coffee bean supply.

1. Farm Dust Sprites until you get 5-10 coffee beans.
2. Plant them and wait for the first harvest.
3. Each harvest drops extra beans, allowing exponential expansion.
4. On Ginger Island, coffee grows year-round.
5. Fill kegs with coffee beans for 420g per coffee (with Artisan).
6. Keep some coffee for drinking (speed buff).

## Summer Crop FAQ

### What Is the Most Profitable Summer Crop?

Starfruit processed into wine with the Artisan profession is the most profitable, at 5,500g net profit per plot per season (2 harvests). Hops processed into Pale Ale with Artisan is a close second at 7,500g per plot but requires significantly more kegs to keep up with daily production.

### Are Blueberries Worth Planting?

Yes, absolutely. Blueberries are the best multi-harvest summer crop, producing 12 berries per plant over 4 harvests from a single 80g seed. Processed into wine or jelly with the Artisan profession, a single blueberry plant generates 2,440g in profit per season.

### Can Red Cabbage Be Planted in Year 1?

Normally no -- Red Cabbage seeds are locked until Year 2. However, completing the "Community Cleanup" special order from Mayor Lewis's bulletin board (added in the 1.5 update) unlocks Red Cabbage seeds at Pierre's in Year 1. This special order requires collecting 100 trash items and 100 driftwood, which is doable but time-consuming.

### Is It Worth Growing Hops Without Kegs?

No. Hops sell for only 25g raw, which is terrible. The entire value of hops lies in processing into Pale Ale (300g base, 420g with Artisan). Do not grow hops unless you have kegs to process them.

### Should I Use Quality Fertilizer or Speed Fertilizer?

It depends on whether you plan to sell raw or process. If selling raw (especially high-value crops like Starfruit), use Quality or Deluxe Fertilizer for higher sell prices. If processing into wine, jelly, pale ale, or pickles, use Speed-Gro or Deluxe Speed-Gro instead, since quality does not affect artisan good prices. More harvests from speed fertilizer means more artisan goods, which is always more profitable.

### How Do I Get Coffee Beans?

Coffee Beans cannot be purchased from any standard shop. They are obtained by: (1) Killing Dust Sprites on Mine levels 40-79 (1% drop chance), (2) Buying from the Traveling Cart when available (rare), (3) Harvesting from existing coffee plants (each harvest has a chance to drop extra beans). Once you have a few plants, they become self-sustaining through the extra bean drops.

### Is Sunflower Worth Growing?

Only if you process it into Sunflower Oil (340g base, 476g with Artisan). Selling sunflowers raw results in a loss. Sunflower Oil requires an Oil Maker, which is crafted from 50 Slime + 25 Hardwood after reaching Farming Level 8. If you have Oil Makers available, sunflowers are a decent processing crop at 828g net profit per plot with Artisan.

### What About Summer Squash?

Summer Squash loses money when sold raw. Only grow it if you plan to process it into pickles (160g base, 224g with Artisan) or use it in cooking. It is not a recommended crop for profit-focused players.

### Can I Plant Crops That Continue Into Fall?

Yes. Corn continues to grow through summer into fall without dying. Coffee Beans also grow in both spring and summer (and year-round on Ginger Island). Both crops will keep producing in fall as long as they were planted during their valid season.

### How Many Kegs Should I Have for Summer?

For Starfruit wine: 2 kegs per Starfruit plot (one for each of the 2 harvests). For Hops Pale Ale: 2 kegs per Hops plot (since hops produce daily and Pale Ale takes about 1.5-2 days to brew). For Blueberries: 3-4 kegs per plot (one per berry per harvest). The more kegs you have, the more profit you can generate. Building 100+ kegs should be a priority before summer.
