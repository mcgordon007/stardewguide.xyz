# Stardew Valley Joja Mart Guide: Complete Overview

Joja Mart is the corporate competitor to Pierre's General Store in Stardew Valley. It offers a different path for community development through the Joja route. This comprehensive guide covers everything about Joja Mart, from prices to membership to the Joja route alternative.

## Store Overview

### Location

Joja Mart is located in Pelican Town:
- East side of town
- Near the bridge to beach
- Large corporate building
- Orange and blue branding

### Store Hours

| Day | Hours |
|-----|-------|
| Monday | 9:00 AM - 11:00 PM |
| Tuesday | 9:00 AM - 11:00 PM |
| Wednesday | 9:00 AM - 11:00 PM |
| Thursday | 9:00 AM - 11:00 PM |
| Friday | 9:00 AM - 11:00 PM |
| Saturday | 9:00 AM - 11:00 PM |
| Sunday | 9:00 AM - 11:00 PM |

**Note**: Joja Mart is open longer hours than Pierre's and never closes, making it convenient but expensive.

## Joja Membership

### Membership Cost

**Cost**: 5,000g
**Effect**: Access to Joja Community Development route

### What Membership Provides

1. **Joja Route Access**: Alternative to Community Center
2. **Discounts**: Minor discounts on some items
3. **Community Development**: Purchase community improvements
4. **Solar Panels**: Unlock solar panel crafting

### Purchasing Membership

**Process**:
1. Visit Joja Mart
2. Talk to Morris (manager)
3. Pay 5,000g
4. Receive membership card

---

## Joja Route for Community Center

### Overview

The Joja route is an alternative to completing the Community Center bundles. Instead of collecting items for bundles, you pay gold to purchase community improvements directly.

### Community Development Form

After purchasing membership, you can buy:

| Improvement | Cost | Equivalent Bundle |
|-------------|------|-------------------|
| Greenhouse | 35,000g | Pantry Bundle |
| Bus Repair | 40,000g | Vault Bundle |
| Minecarts | 15,000g | Boiler Room Bundle |
| Bridge Repair | 25,000g | Vault Bundle |
| Panning | 20,000g | Fish Tank Bundle |
| Movie Theater | 500,000g | Missing Bundle |

### Total Cost

**Total Joja Route Cost**: ~600,000g (including membership)

**Comparison**:
- Community Center: Free (requires item collection)
- Joja Route: ~600,000g (requires gold)

### Advantages of Joja Route

1. **No Bundle Collection**: Don't need to gather specific items
2. **Immediate Access**: Purchase improvements when you have gold
3. **Simple Progression**: Pay gold instead of hunting items
4. **Solar Panels**: Unlock solar panel crafting

### Disadvantages of Joja Route

1. **Expensive**: Costs significantly more gold
2. **No Pierre Wednesday**: Pierre stays closed on Wednesdays
3. **No Bundle Rewards**: Miss bundle-specific rewards
4. **Corporate Theme**: Less community-focused ending

## Solar Panels

### Unlocking Solar Panels

**Requirement**: Complete Joja route

**How to Get**:
1. Complete Joja Community Development
2. Receive solar panel recipe
3. Craft solar panels

### Solar Panel Details

**Crafting**:
- Materials: Refined Quartz (10), Gold Bar (5), Iridium Bar (2)
- Energy: Produces battery packs

**Battery Pack Production**:
- Solar panels generate battery packs
- Used for Iridium Sprinklers
- Used for various crafting
- Renewable energy source

### Solar Panel Benefits

1. **Free Battery Packs**: Generate over time
2. **Iridium Sprinkler Supply**: Essential for sprinklers
3. **Renewable**: Continuous production
4. **Late Game Value**: Very useful

## Conclusion

Joja Mart offers a corporate alternative to Pierre's General Store with higher prices but longer hours. The Joja route provides a gold-based alternative to Community Center completion, costing approximately 600,000g total.

Choose Joja route if you prefer paying gold over collecting bundle items, want solar panels, or have excess money. Choose Community Center route if you enjoy item collection, want bundle rewards, and prefer a community-focused experience. Joja Mart's convenience comes at a price premium, making Pierre's the better choice for regular shopping.
