# Greenhouse Guide

The Greenhouse is one of the most valuable structures in Stardew Valley. It allows you to grow crops from any season year-round, protected from weather and seasonal changes. This guide covers unlocking the greenhouse, optimizing its layout, choosing the best crops, creating a year-round planting plan, and comparing greenhouse farming to outdoor farming.

## Unlocking the Greenhouse

There are two ways to unlock the Greenhouse, depending on whether you chose the Community Center or Joja Mart route.

### Community Center Route

Complete all Pantry bundles in the Community Center. The Pantry bundles include:

| Bundle | Required Items |
|--------|---------------|
| Spring Crops | Parsnip, Green Bean, Cauliflower, Potato |
| Summer Crops | Melon, Tomato, Blueberry, Hot Pepper |
| Fall Crops | Corn, Eggplant, Pumpkin, Yam |
| Quality Crops | Gold-quality Parsnip, Melon, Pumpkin, Corn |
| Animal Bundle | Large Egg, Large Milk, Goat Milk, Wool, Truffle (or any 3 of 5) |

Once all Pantry bundles are complete, the Junimos repair the Greenhouse for free. This is the recommended route for most players.

### Joja Mart Route

Purchase the Greenhouse development project from the Joja Community Development Form for 35,000g. This route is faster but costs significantly more gold.

## Greenhouse Features

- **Year-round growing:** Any crop can grow in any season inside the Greenhouse.
- **Weather protection:** Crops are never destroyed by rain, storms, or seasonal changes.
- **No grass:** Grass does not grow inside the Greenhouse.
- **Sprinklers work:** You can use sprinklers to automate watering.
- **Limited space:** The interior has approximately 120 plantable tiles.
- **Fruit trees can grow:** Fruit trees can be planted inside the Greenhouse and will produce fruit year-round.
- **Bee houses do NOT work:** Bee houses placed inside the Greenhouse will not produce honey.
- **Junimo Huts cannot be placed** inside the Greenhouse.
- **Casks can be placed:** You can place casks inside the Greenhouse for aging wine and cheese.

## Optimal Greenhouse Layout

The most efficient Greenhouse layout uses Iridium Sprinklers, which water the 8 tiles surrounding them (in a 3x3 area minus the center). Each Iridium Sprinkler covers 8 crop tiles.

### Recommended Layout Strategy

1. **Place Iridium Sprinklers** on the center tile of each 3x3 section.
2. **Plant on all 8 surrounding tiles** of each sprinkler.
3. **Leave a walking path** along the edges for access.
4. **Use Quality Fertilizer** on every tile for maximum crop quality.

### Sprinkler Layout Options

| Sprinkler Type | Coverage | Sprinklers Needed for 120 Tiles | Recommended |
|---------------|----------|-------------------------------|-------------|
| Iridium Sprinkler | 8 tiles each | 15 sprinklers | Best option |
| Quality Sprinkler | 8 tiles each | 15 sprinklers | Same coverage, cheaper |
| Deluxe Sprinkler (mod) | 24 tiles each | 5 sprinklers | Not in vanilla game |

### Optimal Tile Count

With careful placement, you can fit approximately 12-15 Iridium Sprinklers in the Greenhouse, covering 96-120 crop tiles. The exact number depends on how you arrange walking paths.

## Best Greenhouse Crops

Since the Greenhouse allows year-round growing, choose crops that provide the best long-term profit.

### Ancient Fruit (Best Overall)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Seed Source | Plant an Ancient Seed (found from artifact spots, fishing, or purchased from the Traveling Merchant) |
| Growth Time | 28 days initial, then regrows every 7 days |
| Base Sell Price | 550g |
| Wine Sell Price | 1,650g (2,310g with Artisan) |
| Iridium Wine (Cask) | 3,300g (4,620g with Artisan) |
| Strategy | Plant once and harvest forever. Each plant produces fruit indefinitely. |

Ancient Fruit is the undisputed king of greenhouse crops. Once planted, it produces fruit every 7 days for the rest of the game. Processing into wine and aging in casks creates the most profitable single product in the game.

### Starfruit (Best Single Harvest)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Seed Cost | 400g (from Oasis in the desert) |
| Growth Time | 13 days |
| Base Sell Price | 750g |
| Wine Sell Price | 2,250g (3,150g with Artisan) |
| Iridium Wine (Cask) | 4,500g (6,300g with Artisan) |
| Strategy | Plant and harvest repeatedly. No regrowth, so replant after each harvest. |

Starfruit wine has the highest single-item sell price in the game. However, since starfruit does not regrow, you must replant after each harvest, which reduces overall efficiency compared to ancient fruit.

### Cranberries (Best Multi-Harvest)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Seed Cost | 240g per seed |
| Growth Time | 7 days initial, then regrows every 5 days |
| Berries Per Harvest | 2 berries |
| Total Harvests Per Season | Up to 5 |
| Jelly Price | 200g per jelly (280g with Artisan) |
| Wine Price | 225g per wine (315g with Artisan) |
| Strategy | Plant once and harvest multiple times. Best with preserves jars. |

## Year-Round Planting Plan

### Strategy 1: Full Ancient Fruit (Recommended)

Fill the entire Greenhouse with Ancient Fruit. This is the simplest and most profitable strategy.

- **Setup:** Plant Ancient Seeds on all available tiles.
- **Maintenance:** Harvest every 7 days. Place kegs around the farm to process wine.
- **Profit:** With 120 ancient fruit plants and Artisan profession, you earn approximately 2,310g per fruit every 7 days = 39,600g per harvest cycle.
- **Annual profit:** ~2,070,000g per year from wine alone (before aging).

### Strategy 2: Mixed Ancient Fruit + Starfruit

Use part of the Greenhouse for Ancient Fruit and part for Starfruit.

- **Ancient Fruit area:** 80 tiles for ancient fruit (regrows every 7 days).
- **Starfruit area:** 40 tiles for starfruit (harvest every 13 days).
- **Profit:** Combines the regrowth advantage of ancient fruit with the high per-item value of starfruit.

### Strategy 3: Seasonal Rotation

Rotate crops by season for variety, though this is less profitable than the above strategies.

- **Spring:** Plant strawberries and potatoes.
- **Summer:** Plant blueberries and melons.
- **Fall:** Plant cranberries and pumpkins.
- **Winter:** Plant winter seeds or leave empty.

## Greenhouse vs Outdoor Farming

| Factor | Greenhouse | Outdoor |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Seasonal restrictions | None | Must match season |
| Weather protection | Full | None |
| Space | ~120 tiles | Virtually unlimited |
| Sprinkler automation | Yes | Yes |
| Fruit trees | Yes (year-round fruit) | Seasonal only |
| Bee houses | No | Yes |
| Casks | Yes | No (cellar only) |
| Best crop | Ancient Fruit | Season-dependent |

## Iridium Sprinkler Layout Guide

### Grid Layout (Most Efficient)

Place sprinklers in a grid pattern with 3 tiles between each sprinkler (center to center). This ensures full coverage with no overlap.

```
S . . S . . S . .
. X X . X X . X X
. X X . X X . X X
. X X . X X . X X
S . . S . . S . .
. X X . X X . X X
. X X . X X . X X
. X X . X X . X X
```

Where S = Sprinkler and X = Crop tile.

## Greenhouse Tips

1. **Ancient Fruit does NOT die** between seasons. Once planted, it produces fruit year-round indefinitely.
2. **You can grow trees** inside the Greenhouse, including fruit trees. Fruit trees produce fruit year-round inside.
3. **The Greenhouse counts as indoors** for the purposes of the game, so seasonal restrictions do not apply.
4. **Bee houses do NOT work** inside the Greenhouse. Place them outdoors near flower crops instead.
5. **Junimo Huts cannot be placed** inside the Greenhouse.
6. **Casks can be placed** inside the Greenhouse for aging wine and cheese.
7. **Do not let the Greenhouse sit empty.** Even basic crops are better than nothing.
8. **Use Deluxe Fertilizer** on every tile for consistent iridium-quality crops.
9. **Plant Ancient Fruit as soon as possible.** The earlier you plant, the more you benefit from its indefinite regrowth.
10. **Process all greenhouse crops.** Never sell raw crops when you can make wine, jelly, or pickles instead.
